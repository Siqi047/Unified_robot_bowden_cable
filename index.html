Optimization-Based Motion Synthesis for Unified Manipulation in Robot Hand-Arm Systems with Bowden Cable Transmission

Lingyun Chen*, Siqi Yuan*, Junnan Li
Technical University of Munich
Munich Institute of Robotics and Machine Intelligence
*Indicates Equal Contribution
Paper Supplementary Code arXiv

Our optimization framework enables stable force transmission in unified hand-arm robot systems through Bowden cable modeling and multi-objective trajectory optimization.

Abstract

Unified hand–arm robot systems present ongoing challenges, especially when applying tendon-driven five-finger systems using Bowden cables. We address optimization-based motion synthesis for hand–arm robot systems by modeling and optimizing a single Bowden-cable transmission mounted on a Franka Emika Panda arm and its end effector. The shape of the Bowden cable, which varies with the robot arm's configuration, influences the characteristics of force transmission and thereby affects the grasping force. Our trajectory optimization proposes a nonlinear optimization framework to minimize the force change of a Bowden cable while ensuring robot arm trajectory tracking. Although we instantiated this project with a single cable, the formulation generalizes to unified hand-arm robot control, where a multi-tendon-driven, multi-finger hand is actuated via Bowden transmissions. The results provide an optimization foundation for more dexterous hand-arm systems, as well as coordinated manipulation with optimization-based design of unified hand–arm robotic systems.
